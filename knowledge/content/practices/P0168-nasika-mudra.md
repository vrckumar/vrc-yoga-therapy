# Nasika Mudra

Nasika Mudra is the hand position used to regulate airflow through one nostril at a time during
selected pranayama practices. In this library it appears most often in alternate-nostril or
single-nostril breathing instructions.

## 1. Core Hand Position

- Fold the index and middle fingers toward the palm, or rest them lightly at the brow depending on
  the teaching style.
- Use the thumb to close the right nostril.
- Use the ring finger and little finger to close the left nostril.
- Keep the shoulders relaxed and the elbow supported as much as possible.

## 2. Used In

- [Nadi Suddhi Pranayama](P0069-nadi-suddhi.md)
- [Nadi Shuddhi](P0070-nadi-shuddhi.md)
- [Surya Anuloma Viloma](P0073-surya-anuloma-viloma.md)
- some teaching variations of [Kapalabhati](P0072-kapalabhati.md)

## 3. Practical Notes

- The pressure should be gentle, not crushing.
- The non-working shoulder should stay soft.
- The purpose is airflow control, not hand strain.
