# Sukhasana (Easy Pose)

*Sukha* (Sanskrit) — ease, comfort, pleasantness. Sukhasana is the simplest cross-legged seated
posture used throughout the library as an accessible base for breathing practices, chanting, and
meditation when [Padmasana](P0166-padmasana.md) is unnecessary or not available.

## 1. Core Position

- Sit cross-legged with the pelvis slightly elevated if needed.
- Keep the spine erect without rigidity.
- Rest the hands on the knees or thighs.
- Relax the jaw, shoulders, and abdomen.
- Maintain an easy, sustainable breath.

## 2. Why It Is Commonly Used

- More accessible than lotus-based sitting.
- Suitable for pranayama, humming, mantra, and seated awareness practices.
- Useful when knee or hip restrictions make deeper seated postures impractical.

## 3. Cautions

- If the lower back rounds heavily, sit on a folded support.
- If cross-legged sitting aggravates the hips or knees, substitute with [Vajrasana](P0154-vajrasana.md)
  or supported chair sitting.
