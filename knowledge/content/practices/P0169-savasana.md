# Savasana (Shavasana / Corpse Pose)

Savasana is the foundational supine relaxation posture used throughout the library for rest,
integration, autonomic settling, and the delivery of guided relaxation methods such as
[IRT](P0125-irt.md), [QRT](P0124-qrt.md), and [DRT](P0123-drt.md).

## 1. Core Position

- Lie flat on the back.
- Let the feet fall apart naturally.
- Rest the arms slightly away from the body.
- Allow the palms to face upward or rest neutrally.
- Keep the face, jaw, eyes, and abdomen relaxed.

## 2. Why It Matters

- Provides the baseline recovery posture after effort.
- Helps settle breath and heart rate.
- Gives a stable body position for awareness, release, and guided relaxation.
- Serves as the default resting position between or after many supine practices.

## 3. Common Uses In This Library

- post-practice rest after asana or kriya
- setup position for [IRT](P0125-irt.md), [QRT](P0124-qrt.md), and [DRT](P0123-drt.md)
- calming version of breath or sound practices when sitting is not practical

## 4. Cautions

- If lying flat aggravates the low back, knees may be bent or supported.
- If reflux, breathlessness, or pregnancy limits flat supine lying, use an adapted supported position.
