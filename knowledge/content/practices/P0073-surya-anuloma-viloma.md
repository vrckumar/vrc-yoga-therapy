# Surya Anuloma Viloma (Right Nostril Solar Breathing)

*Surya* (Sanskrit) — sun; *Anuloma* — with the natural flow; *Viloma* — against the natural flow. In yogic physiology, the right nostril is the *Pingala Nadi* — the solar channel, associated with heat, activation, the sympathetic nervous system, and the left cerebral hemisphere. Surya Anuloma Viloma practises breathing exclusively through the right nostril, deliberately channelling the solar pranic current.

This is the stimulating counterpart to the cooling Chandra (left nostril) practices. Where Chandra pranayamas calm and cool, Surya Anuloma Viloma energises, warms, and metabolically activates — making it the essential pranayama in the IAYT protocols for obesity, lethargy, and depression.

**Evidence note:** This page relies primarily on traditional teaching and the S-VYASA source corpus. External unilateral-nostril-breathing research is small and mixed. It supports cautious discussion of autonomic and oxygen-consumption effects, but not strong disease-treatment claims for this single practice.

## 1. Technique

**Starting position (Sthiti):** Sit comfortably in [Vajrasana](P0154-vajrasana.md), [Padmasana](P0166-padmasana.md), or [Sukhasana](P0167-sukhasana.md) with spine erect. Alternatively, any stable seated position.

**Hand position:** [Nasika Mudra](P0168-nasika-mudra.md) with the right hand — fold the index and middle fingers into the palm, keeping the thumb, ring finger, and little finger extended. Elbow rests on the upper abdomen to avoid arm fatigue.

**The practice:**
1. Use the ring and little fingers of the right hand to close the **left nostril** completely. This closure is maintained throughout the entire practice.
2. Inhale slowly and fully through the **right nostril** alone.
3. Exhale slowly and completely through the **right nostril** alone (left remains closed throughout).
4. This inhalation-exhalation sequence through the right nostril constitutes **one round**.
5. The left nostril does not participate in any phase.

**Pace:** The breath should be slow, smooth, and controlled — not forced. The focus is on maintaining a consistent right-nostril-only pattern.

**Dosage in IAYT protocols:** 27 rounds, four times daily for obesity management. In general practice, 9 rounds per session.

**Important note on laterality:** This practice is the right-nostril equivalent of Chandra Bhedana (which is inhale through left, exhale through left). Unlike [Nadi Suddhi](P0069-nadi-suddhi.md), where nostrils alternate, here the right nostril is both entry and exit for every breath.

## 2. Anatomical Activation

### Primary structures engaged

**Right nasal passage and turbinates:** The right inferior, middle, and superior turbinates receive the entire airflow. The nasal mucosa on the right side is richly supplied with sympathetic nerve fibres that respond to airflow — increased airflow through the right nostril directly stimulates the right nasal sympathetic network.

**Nasika Mudra hand position:** The ring and little fingers maintain sustained closure of the left nostril against the alae nasi. This closure must be gentle but complete — any leakage of air through the left nostril reduces the specificity of the practice.

**Right cerebral hemisphere connections (via olfactory pathway):** Olfactory signals from the right nasal epithelium travel through the right olfactory bulb to the right olfactory cortex, which projects to the ipsilateral (right) hemisphere. However, the key autonomic connection is contralateral: right nostril airflow predominantly activates the **left hemisphere** through nasal-cerebral reflex arcs studied in the Swami Vivekananda Yoga University research tradition.

**Sympathetic nervous system pathways:** The sphenopalatine ganglion — located in the pterygopalatine fossa behind the nose — serves as a relay station. Right nasal airflow stimulates specific sympathetic pathways through this ganglion to the thoracolumbar sympathetic chain.

**Hypothalamus:** Via the olfactory-limbic-hypothalamic pathway, right nostril breathing tonically stimulates the lateral hypothalamus, the sympathetic activating centre.

## 3. Physiological Effects

### Sympathetic nervous system activation — the primary mechanism

The right nostril is the *Pingala Nadi* in yogic physiology — the solar channel. Modern physiological research has validated that right nostril breathing produces measurably greater sympathetic activation than left nostril breathing:

**1. Nasal-cerebral sympathetic reflex arc**
The nasal mucosa is supplied by both sympathetic and parasympathetic fibres. When airflow is directed exclusively through the right nostril, the mechanical stimulation of airflow and the olfactory signals preferentially activate the sympathetic-linked pathways through the right sphenopalatine ganglion. This signal reaches the hypothalamus and activates the sympathoadrenal axis — stimulating release of catecholamines (norepinephrine and epinephrine) from the adrenal medulla.

**2. Raised metabolic rate and thermogenesis**
Catecholamine release increases the basal metabolic rate by stimulating beta-adrenergic receptors on adipocytes and skeletal muscle cells:
- **Adipose tissue:** Norepinephrine activates beta-3 adrenergic receptors on fat cells, stimulating lipolysis (the breakdown of triglycerides into free fatty acids). This is the specific mechanism by which Surya Anuloma Viloma contributes to fat mobilisation in obesity management.
- **Skeletal muscle:** Increased sympathetic tone raises skeletal muscle metabolic activity — increasing calorie consumption even at rest.
- **Brown adipose tissue:** Sympathetic stimulation activates uncoupling protein-1 (UCP-1) in brown fat, producing heat directly (non-shivering thermogenesis).

**3. Heat generation (solar/warming quality)**
The combination of raised metabolic rate and increased muscle tone generates body heat — the traditional description of Surya pranayama as warming is physiologically accurate. This warming effect:
- Raises core body temperature slightly, which is associated with increased immune activation
- Counteracts the reduced core temperature associated with hypothyroidism and obesity
- Improves peripheral circulation, reducing the cold extremities common in metabolic syndrome

**4. Mental activation and alertness — left hemisphere dominance**
Some source traditions and small studies discuss hemisphere-linked alertness effects with right nostril breathing. These ideas are interesting but should be treated cautiously in public-facing copy because the evidence base is limited and not fully consistent.

For low-energy or low-motivation states, the source rationale is that a stimulating breath practice may increase subjective alertness and engagement. That should be presented as supportive rather than as a stand-alone depression treatment.

**5. Obesity and weight management protocol rationale**
The IAYT obesity protocol uses 27 rounds four times daily as a specific therapeutic dose for weight management. The combined effects of:
- Increased basal metabolic rate (sympathetic activation)
- Lipolysis (fat mobilisation)
- Reduced appetite (sympathetic suppression of digestive activity)
- Mental activation reducing compensatory eating behaviour
…work synergistically to support weight reduction alongside dietary and asana interventions.

## 4. Kosha Level

**Primary:** Pranamaya Kosha — the direct activation of Pingala Nadi (the solar pranic channel) through sustained right nostril breathing. In the yogic energy model, the Pingala Nadi runs from the right side of Muladhara Chakra upward through the right nostril — Surya Anuloma Viloma enlivens this channel, increasing *tejas* (the fire principle) and *ojas* (vital energy) throughout the system.

**Secondary:** Annamaya Kosha — the physical metabolic effects (raised metabolic rate, thermogenesis, lipolysis) are primary at the physical body level and constitute the main therapeutic mechanism in obesity and metabolic conditions.

The practice works from Pranamaya (pranic channel activation) downward to Annamaya (metabolic effect), and upward to Manomaya (mental activation and alertness) — demonstrating the bi-directional energetic model of yoga therapy.

| **Diabetes Mellitus** | Included mainly when metabolic deconditioning and obesity are part of the broader clinical picture. |
| **Sluggish digestion / Constipation** | Sympathetic-mediated gastric acid secretion and peristaltic stimulation — improve digestive fire (*agni*). |

## 6. Contraindications

- **Hypertension (moderate to severe)** — sympathetic activation raises blood pressure. Avoid if BP > 140/90 unless under medical supervision. Use Chandra (left nostril) practices instead.
- **Fever and inflammatory conditions** — the warming, thermogenic effect is contraindicated during active fever or acute inflammation.
- **Ischaemic Heart Disease (IHD) — active** — increased sympathetic tone increases cardiac demand. Avoid until stable.
- **Anxiety and panic disorders** — sympathetic activation can exacerbate anxiety states. Use Bhramari and Nadi Suddhi instead.
- **Hyperthyroidism** — already in a hypermetabolic state; further sympathetic stimulation is contraindicated.
- **Pregnancy** — avoid stimulating practices during pregnancy. Use only calming pranayamas.
- **During menstruation** — avoid strongly stimulating practices; use gentle bilateral breathing instead.

## 7. Variations & Progressions

**Chandra Anuloma Viloma (left nostril only):** The cooling, calming, parasympathetic-dominant counterpart. Indicated for hypertension, anxiety, insomnia, and inflammatory conditions. Same technique but left nostril open, right closed throughout.

**Surya Bhedana:** Inhale through right nostril, exhale through left nostril. A variation that uses the stimulating quality of right inhalation but allows balanced exhalation. Slightly less strongly stimulating than Surya Anuloma Viloma.

**Chandra Bhedana:** Inhale through left, exhale through right. Cooling variant.

**Surya Anuloma Viloma Kapalabhati:** The rapid cleansing breath performed through the right nostril only — the most strongly stimulating variant. Used in advanced obesity management protocols.

**Ratio introduction:** Once basic Surya Anuloma Viloma is established, a Puraka:Recaka ratio of 1:1 (equal inhalation and exhalation) is introduced, followed by a 1:2 ratio in advanced practice.

**Combined protocol (obesity):** Surya Anuloma Viloma is always combined with Kapalabhati, Bhastrika, Agnisara, and dietary modification in the IAYT obesity protocol — it is not used in isolation.
