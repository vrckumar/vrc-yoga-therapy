# Padmasana (Lotus Pose)

*Padma* (Sanskrit) — lotus. Padmasana is the classical cross-legged meditative posture in which
each foot is placed on the opposite thigh. Across the library, it is referenced mainly as a stable
seat for pranayama, meditation, chanting, and selected arm-balance practices.

## 1. Core Position

- Sit with spine erect and pelvis balanced.
- Bend one leg and place the foot high on the opposite thigh.
- Bend the second leg and place that foot on the opposite thigh as well.
- Knees descend toward the floor as hip external rotation increases.
- Hands may rest on the knees or thighs in a comfortable mudra.

## 2. Why It Is Referenced So Often

- Provides a compact and steady base for meditation.
- Keeps the trunk upright for breath practices.
- Minimises shifting during chanting, contemplation, and seated concentration.
- Serves as a traditional base position for practices such as [Tulasana](P0096-tulasana.md) and
  some classical forms of [Matsyasana](P0081-matsyasana.md).

## 3. Cautions

- Do not force the knees into the posture.
- Hip mobility should create the shape; the knee should not absorb twisting stress.
- If lotus is not comfortable, use [Sukhasana](P0167-sukhasana.md) or [Vajrasana](P0154-vajrasana.md).
